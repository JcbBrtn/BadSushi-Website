The Trials of Mastery

There is a founded technique that can help you master anything, given the right motivation, time, and resources. Just doing it. If you want to be good at something, there is only one thing you need to do. Do It. Everyone is a master at finding mistakes. So they will let you know they "are better than you" because you made a mistake they can see. Well guess what. Everyone who has ever mastered something has started as a beginner, making beginner mistakes. We should not be punished for the mistakes we make trying something new. We should be rewarded for attempting to better ourselves for trying something new. Yes, the feedback is nice. But feedback is more appreciated from an eye that will see beyond the basics that anyone is able to see as a mistake. This trained eye will understand the efforts being put in, and see that as more appreciated effort than the blatant mistake that it may be.

While trying anything new for the first time, we are able to get to a certant level on our own, because we can look back and see the mistakes we have made along the way. As long as we have the image in our head, we can compare it to relatity and make adjustments accordingly. Therefore, the only limitiation that we have is the one that we hold in our own mind. That revilation will take us to new heights. The teacher, or critic will give us new ideas to shoot for, and help us avoid pitfalls along the way. But ultimatley, self reflection, honest self truth, and time is what is needed for mastery.
